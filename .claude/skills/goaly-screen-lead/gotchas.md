# Screen Lead — Gotchas

- **No calls in reply drafts** — [Owner] hates calls. Never offer, suggest, or hint at scheduling a call. Email only. Let the lead suggest a call if they want one.
- **Duplicate check first** — Before creating a client folder or contact, grep `notion-mirror/clients/` and `notion-mirror/contacts/` for the lead's name/company. They may already exist from a prior interaction.
- **Red flags to watch for** — "Quick call to discuss", scope ambiguity with urgency ("ASAP + ambitious"), no budget signal, title shifting across platforms, wants free prototype before paying.
- **Research cost escalation** — Start with local search (qmd, Grep), then free tools (gog, curl, sherlock), then paid APIs (Exa ~$0.005/query), then expensive tools (perplexity_research ~$1+). Only escalate for high-value leads.
- **Phase 3 is gated** — Never create client folders, draft replies, or create Notion tasks until [Owner] explicitly confirms interest. The research report stands on its own.
- **Retainer-first framing** — Any reply draft must angle toward ongoing retainers, not fixed-scope projects. "The way I work is ongoing retainers rather than fixed-scope projects."
- **Budget question is mandatory** — Every qualifying reply must ask about confirmed budget. Don't let it slide.
- **Strip buzzwords in analysis** — "AI-powered marketing mission control" becomes "a dashboard that pulls analytics from multiple ad platforms." Present what they actually need in plain language.
